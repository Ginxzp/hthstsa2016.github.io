CAREER AND TECHNICAL EDUCATION

U.S. News & World Report named High Technology High School the #1 STEM school in the entire nation. Specializing in science, mathematics, engineering, and technology, High Tech offers a multitude of Project Lead The Way courses that will fully prepare its students for a bright future in any one of these specific fields. From freshman to senior, the curriculum always remains challenging, yet rewarding at the same time.

FRESHMAN

A FRESH START

When you first walk into High Technology High School as an incoming freshman, you are immediately immersed in a purely engineering environment. With two unique engineering classes, being "Introduction to Engineering Design" and "Computer Integrated Manufacturing", students are given a myriad of opportunities to apply themselves in this field and take off on the journey of finding their inner engineer.

SOPHOMORE

TAKING IT A LEVEL HIGHER

Sophomore year at High Technology High School allows each student to enhance their knowledge of the engineering field, with two more insightful courses: "Principles of Engineering" and "Computer Programming for Engineers." Each course delves into the attributes of mechanincal engineering, programming, and real-world applications of the Project Lead The Way design process.

JUNIOR

CHOOSING THE RIGHT PATH

Choosing the right field in engineering is always a hard decision. However, junior year at High Technology High School offers 4 specific technology electives that will fulfill the desire to excel in a specific field or simply help each student realize their place in the vast world of STEM. Consisting of "Computer Science and Software Engineering" and "Civil Engineering and Architecture", our students are completely able to map out their futures.

SENIOR

APPLY YOUR KNOWLEDGE

Senior Year. All the knowledge from 3 years of experience at High Technology High School culminates into the rigorous curriculum of Engineering Design & Development. Having learned nearly every aspect of PLTW's engineering curriculum, students are required to apply their immense knowledge and invent new things. Additionally, seniors are given the opportunity to perform research experiments.
