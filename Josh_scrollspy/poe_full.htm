Principles of Engineering Design


With Freshman year completed, High Tech students move on through their education in engineering to Principles of Engineering Design. The class teaches students about simple machines, the mathematics behind simple engineering concepts (like stress, simple machines, etc.). The students also learn about engineering ethics and recycling, concepts important to grasp so that in their careers, they can be not only efficient and successful but responsible engineers.

The second half of the year is mainly spent on using VEX robotics and learning the programming language RobotC. These skills are mastered as different sensors, motors, and equipment are handled, programmed, and applied to complete tasks. In addition, team projects help reestablish the theme that teamwork is an essential skill for an engineer. For example, all students work with a group to build a tower that is 8 feet tall and can support a soccer ball for at least 24 hours out of only newspaper and tape. All of these skills and teamwork lessons ultimately prepare the students to venture into their Junior year electives.

Mr. Christopher Hanas

Mr. Christopher Hanas teaches Computer Science and Engineering and Principles of Engineering at High Technology High School.

"POE is focused on introducing students to the fundamental concepts in mechanical, civil, electrical and chemical engineering. This is a part of the the Project Lead the Way pre-engineering program for students who are interested in pursuing an engineering or science degree when at college. As an engineering course, the topics focus heavily on scientific concepts and applied mathematics such as: The Engineering Design Process, Vectors, Energy & Power (Mechanisms, Energy Sources, Energy Applications), Materials & Structures (Statics, Material Properties, Material Testing), Control Systems (Robot Control, Fluid Power), and Kinematics & Statistics."
When asked about skills the students learn in POE, Mr. Hanas replied: "In place of unit tests, POE uses group-based unit projects to assess the students’ design and analysis capabilities. These projects require the students to research, brainstorm, prototype and test their designs. In addition to a working design model, the students are also responsible for submitting a final design report and, in some cases, making a verbal presentation to the class. Due to the project scope, the successful team must demonstrate strong collaboration among all members."
